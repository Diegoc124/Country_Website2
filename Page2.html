Día de los Muertos (Day of the Dead) is a Mexican celebration on November 1st and 2nd, honoring deceased loved ones. Families create altars, offer food, and celebrate with music, marigolds, and sugar skulls, believing the spirits return to join the living.

Source

Las Posadas is a Mexican tradition celebrated from December 16th to 24th, reenacting Mary and Joseph's search for shelter. Participants go door-to-door singing carols, ending with a festive gathering of food, piñatas, and prayers.
